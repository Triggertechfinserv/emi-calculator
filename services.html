What is EMI? Complete Guide to Loan Repayments

Published on September 26, 2025 | By TechFinserv Team

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. Each EMI has two parts: a principal component that reduces your outstanding amount, and an interest component that is the cost of borrowing.

Understanding the Interest Rate

The interest rate directly influences your EMI. Even a 0.5% difference in rate can significantly change the total interest paid over long tenures.

Types of Interest

1) Fixed Interest Rate

Rate remains the same throughout the tenure, so your EMI does not change.

2) Floating (Variable) Interest Rate

Rate moves with market benchmarks. EMIs may rise or fallâ€”often cheaper over the long run.

3) Reducing Balance Interest

Interest is charged on the outstanding principal only. As you repay, the interest portion declines.

4) Flat Interest Rate

Interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire tenure. Simple but costlier.

Interest Component of EMI

At the start, a larger share of EMI goes to interest because the outstanding balance is high.

What is Part Payment?

Part payment means paying an additional lump sum besides regular EMIs. This reduces outstanding principal, lowering future interest.

What is Foreclosure?

Foreclosure means clearing the entire loan before the scheduled end date. It cuts future interest and makes you debt-free sooner.
