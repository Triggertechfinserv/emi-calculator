What Is an Unauthorized Credit Card Transaction?

An unauthorized credit card transaction is any payment that you did not approve. This includes:

  • Online purchases you never made
  • Wallet or app payments done without consent
  • International transactions you did not authorize
  • Transactions showing OTP usage that you never approved

Important: OTP authentication alone does not automatically prove that you authorized the transaction.

Why Credit Card Fraud Is Increasing in India

Credit card fraud cases are rising due to:

  • Phishing emails, SMS, and fake websites
  • Malware-infected smartphones
  • SIM-swap fraud
  • Data breaches at merchant platforms
  • Card details saved on insecure apps

Most victims never intentionally share OTP or card details. Fraud often happens silently without the customer realizing it.

Step 1: Block Your Credit Card Immediately

After blocking your card:

  • Clearly state that the transaction was unauthorized
  • Ask the bank to register it as a fraud case, not a merchant dispute
  • Request written confirmation via SMS or email

This step activates RBI consumer protection timelines.

RBI Zero Liability Principle Explained Simply

According to consumer protection guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), customers are not liable for losses caused by unauthorized electronic transactions if there is no customer negligence.

Banks often reject fraud claims by stating that OTP was used. OTP usage alone does not prove customer negligence.

OTP interception can occur due to:

  • SIM-swap fraud
  • Spyware or malware
  • Compromised devices or networks

Unless the bank can prove negligence, liability remains with the bank.

Step 1: Write to the Bank‚Äôs Grievance Officer

  • Quote your complaint reference number

For serious fraud cases, you may also report it on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.

Does Credit Card Fraud Affect Your Credit Score?

No. Unauthorized transactions do not affect your credit score if reported correctly. Only unpaid legitimate dues impact your credit profile.
