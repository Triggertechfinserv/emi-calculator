What is EMI? Complete Guide to Loan Repayments

Published on September 26, 2025 · By TechFinserv Team

At a glance:
  • EMI = fixed monthly payment with principal + interest.
  • Early EMIs are interest-heavy; later EMIs are principal-heavy.
  • Part-payments & foreclosure reduce total interest.

Understanding the Interest Rate

The interest rate directly influences your EMI. Even a 0.5% difference can significantly change total interest over long tenures.

Types of Interest

Quick tip: Prefer reducing-balance loans over flat-rate loans—they usually cost less over time.

1) Fixed Interest Rate

Rate stays the same throughout the tenure, so your EMI does not change. Great for predictable budgeting.

2) Floating (Variable) Interest Rate

Moves with market benchmarks. EMIs may rise or fall—often cheaper long term but less predictable.

3) Reducing Balance Interest

Interest is charged on the outstanding principal only. Over time, the interest part declines and principal part increases.

4) Flat Interest Rate

Calculated on the original loan amount for the entire tenure—typically costlier than reducing-balance.

EMI Formula (with Example)

The standard EMI formula for reducing-balance loans:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1)
  • P = Principal (loan amount)
  • r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate ÷ 12)
  • n = Total number of EMIs (months)

Example: P = ₹10,00,000; annual rate = 10% ⇒ r = 0.10/12; n = 120 months (10 years). Plug into the formula to compute EMI. Or use our EMI Calculator instantly.

Interest vs Principal Components

Early in a loan, a larger share of EMI goes to interest because outstanding balance is high. Later, more of your EMI goes toward principal.

What is Part Payment?

Part payment means paying a lump sum besides regular EMIs. This reduces outstanding principal, lowering future interest. After a part payment, you can reduce EMI or keep EMI same and shorten the tenure.

Example: If your balance is ₹7,00,000 and you part-pay ₹1,00,000, future interest is computed on ₹6,00,000.

What is Foreclosure?

Foreclosure is clearing the entire outstanding loan before the scheduled end date. It cuts future interest and makes you debt-free sooner. Check prepayment/foreclosure fees and compare with expected interest savings.

How to Save Interest (Actionable)

  • Increase EMI when income rises—small increases reduce tenure notably.
  • Early part-payments have the biggest impact (more interest saved).
  • Balance transfer to a lower rate if the spread is meaningful after fees.
  • Reduce tenure instead of EMI after part-payment to maximize savings.
